    Offensive Attack Strategy

    Target Fenerbahce's defensive weaknesses: their last-5-game defensive stats show significant drop-off — opponents are shooting 49.8% FG and 45.0% from three over the last 5 games (vs. season averages of 44.2% FG and 32.7% 3P%). Their transition defense also allows 9.3 fast-break points per game. The primary targets on offense are Nando De Colo (limited lateral quickness), Khem Birch (struggles in space away from the rim), and Talen Horton Tucker (inconsistent defensive discipline). Push pace early — Fenerbahce prefers a controlled, medium tempo and is more vulnerable when forced to defend in transition.

    3 Talen Horton Tucker (#8) Run multiple actions at him off-ball — test his awareness and force him into consecutive defensive reads

    Top 5 Emphasis Points

    1 — Contain Wade Baldwin IV in Pick & Roll

    Baldwin is the offensive engine (14.4 PPG, 5.5 APG). He prefers right-side PnR with Birch rolling to the rim. Use Hedge/Show coverages to disrupt his rhythm. Force him left, shade his driving lane, and mix coverages to keep him off balance. He is a streaky shooter (27.7% 3P%) — do not go fully under screens but be disciplined, not reckless.

    2 — Close Out Hard on Biberovic, Never Relax

    Biberovic is their most efficient 3-point shooter (39.2% on 5 attempts per game; 60% 3P% in the last 5 games). He relocates well off screens and has a quick trigger. Top-lock him on the weak side, be physical on any screen action, and force him to dribble rather than shoot.

    3 — Exploit De Colo and Horton Tucker Defensively

    De Colo has limited lateral quickness and is often protected within the scheme. Force him into on-ball situations via PnR or ISO. Horton Tucker can be exploited with sequential off-ball actions that test his reads. These are the two most attackable defenders in their rotation.

    4 — Attack Khem Birch Away from the Rim

    Birch is their primary roll man and rim protector, but he struggles when pulled out of the paint. Use pick-and-pop actions and short roll reads to draw him away from his comfort zone. With Birch out of position, the paint becomes accessible.

    5 — Push Pace and Attack Transition

    Fenerbahce allows 9.3 fast-break points per game and has gone 0-5 in their last 5 games. They prefer to control tempo. Generate pace off defensive rebounds and made baskets — run secondary break before their defense sets up, particularly targeting De Colo who is slow recovering. They concede 2.6 transition fouls per game leading to free throws.

    Summary

    Fenerbahce Beko Istanbul is a structured, defense-first team under Jasikevičius that has fallen sharply in recent form (0–5 in last 5 games, dropping from 3rd to 5th). While their season record (23-14, 62.2%) reflects genuine quality, their defense has badly deteriorated in the past month — opponents are scoring 88 points per game in this stretch and shooting 45% from three. The three things that must be executed to win:
    1) contain Wade Baldwin IV in the PnR and force him left, disrupting his rhythm before he can build momentum;
    2) relentlessly attack Nando De Colo and Khem Birch in favorable matchups — these are the two most exploitable defenders in their scheme; 3) push tempo every chance available, denying Fenerbahce the controlled, half-court game that is their comfort zone. This team is winnable against right now — their confidence is low and their defense has significant cracks. Execute the game plan, attack the matchups, and take care of the ball against their pressure.
